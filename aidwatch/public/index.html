What AidWatch is

AidWatch is a search engine for accountability. It indexes cases where official oversight bodies — the World Bank, UN oversight offices, donor government auditors, development banks, courts, and verified investigative journalism outlets — have documented misconduct involving humanitarian and development organizations.

Intended users include: institutional donors conducting due diligence; investigative journalists researching the sector; researchers studying aid accountability; civil society organizations tracking organizational track records; and the general public.

What AidWatch is not

AidWatch does not make independent allegations. It does not investigate or accuse. Every case is sourced from an official document or verified reporting, with a direct link. The presence of a case reflects the finding of the cited source only.

⚠️ If you believe information in this database is inaccurate or outdated, please contact us with the source document. We review all corrections promptly.

How to submit a case

AidWatch accepts submissions of documented cases from official sources. To submit, provide: the organization name, a link to the official source document, and a brief description. We do not accept unverified allegations or anonymous tips without documentation.

Methodology & risk scores

Risk scores are calculated based on: number of documented cases; severity of findings; recency; case status (open vs. closed); and whether the organization has taken documented remedial action. Scores are indicators only and do not represent an independent assessment by AidWatch.
