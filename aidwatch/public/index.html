About AidWatch

AidWatch is a free, non-partisan accountability tool that aggregates publicly available documented cases of fraud, corruption, financial misconduct, and abuse in the humanitarian and development sector.

What AidWatch is

AidWatch is a search engine for accountability. It indexes cases where official oversight bodies — the World Bank, UN oversight offices, donor government auditors, development banks, courts, and verified investigative journalism outlets — have documented misconduct involving humanitarian and development organizations.

The tool is intended for use by: institutional donors conducting due diligence on partners; investigative journalists researching the sector; researchers and academics studying aid accountability; civil society organizations tracking organizational track records; and the general public.

What AidWatch is not

AidWatch does not make independent allegations. It does not investigate or accuse. Every case in the database is sourced from an official document or verified reporting with a direct link. The presence of a case in this database reflects the finding of the cited source only.

AidWatch is not a blacklist. Many organizations have addressed documented issues through accountability processes, and case status is reflected in the database.

⚠️ If you believe information in this database is inaccurate or outdated, please contact us with the source document. We are committed to accuracy and will review all corrections promptly.

How to submit a case

AidWatch accepts submissions of documented cases from official sources. To submit, provide:

  • The name of the organization
  • A link to the official source document (audit report, court record, debarment notice, etc.)
  • A brief description of the documented finding

We do not accept unverified allegations, anonymous tips without documentation, or cases based solely on media reports without an underlying official document.

Methodology

Cases are indexed from: official audit and investigation reports from UN oversight bodies; donor government OIG reports and annual reviews; development bank integrity investigations and debarment notices; court records and criminal convictions; verified investigative journalism from established outlets with documented source material.

Risk scores are calculated based on: number of documented cases; severity of findings (financial amounts, type of misconduct); recency of cases; case status (open vs. closed); and whether the organization has taken documented remedial action.

AidWatch is a non-partisan accountability tool. All cases are sourced from publicly available official documents. AidWatch does not make independent allegations. Inclusion in this database reflects documented findings by the cited source only. Organizations with resolved cases may request review of their status.
